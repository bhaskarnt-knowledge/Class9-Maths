Number Systems – Learnings

Key Concepts

  • Rational numbers: Can be written as p/q, where p, q are integers and q ≠ 0.
  • Irrational numbers: Cannot be written as p/q (with p, q integers, q ≠ 0).
  • Real numbers: All rational and irrational numbers together.
  • Decimal expansion: Rational: terminating or recurring; Irrational: non-terminating, non-recurring.
  • Operations: Rational ± irrational = irrational; Rational × irrational (≠0) = irrational.
  • Rationalisation: Multiply numerator and denominator by the conjugate to remove surds from denominators.
  • Laws of exponents:
    ap × aq = ap+q,
    (ap)q = apq,
    ap × bp = (ab)p,
    ap / aq = ap-q

Illustrative Example

Express 0.123̅ in the form p/q:
Let x = 0.123123123...
1000x = 123.123123...
1000x - x = 123.123123... - 0.123123... = 123
999x = 123 ⇒ x = 123/999 = 41/333

Tip: Decimal expansion helps classify numbers as rational or irrational.

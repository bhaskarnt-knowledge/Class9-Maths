Key Concepts at a Glance

Rational Numbers
Numbers expressible as p/q, where p, q are integers and q ≠ 0. Decimal expansion is terminating or repeating.
e.g. 3/4 = 0.75, 2/3 = 0.666…
Irrational Numbers
Numbers not expressible as p/q. Decimal expansion is non-terminating, non-repeating.
e.g. √2 = 1.414213..., π = 3.14159...
Real Numbers
All rational and irrational numbers together form the set of real numbers.
e.g. -7, 0, 1/2, √3, π
Whole & Natural Numbers
Natural: Counting numbers from 1. Whole: Natural numbers plus 0.
N: 1,2,3...
W: 0,1,2,3...
Integers
All positive & negative whole numbers, including 0.
..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ...

Key Formulas

  • Rational: p/q, q ≠ 0
  • Product of surds: √a × √b = √(ab)
  • Quotient of surds: √a / √b = √(a/b)
  • Conjugate rationalisation: 1/(a-b) × (a+b)/(a+b) = (a+b)/(a²-b²)
  • Laws of exponents:
    • ap × aq = ap+q
    • (ap)q = apq
    • ap × bp = (ab)p
    • ap / aq = ap-q

What is a rational number?
A number that can be written as p/q, where p and q are integers and q ≠ 0. Example: 3/4, –2, 0.25
What is an irrational number?
A number that cannot be written as p/q; its decimal expansion is non-terminating and non-repeating. Example: √2, π
Can the sum of two irrational numbers be rational?
Yes, sometimes. Example: (2 + √7) + (2 – √7) = 4 (rational)
Can the product of two irrational numbers be rational?
Yes, sometimes. Example: (2 + √7) × (2 – √7) = 4 – 7 = –3 (rational)
What is always the result of adding a rational and an irrational number?
Always irrational. Example: 3 + √2 is irrational.

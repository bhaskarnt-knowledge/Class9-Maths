Chapter 1 – Number Systems

Key Concepts

  • Rational Numbers: Numbers that can be written as p/q, where p and q are integers and q ≠ 0.
  • Irrational Numbers: Numbers that cannot be written as p/q (with p, q integers, q ≠ 0), e.g., √2, π.
  • Real Numbers: The set of all rational and irrational numbers.
  • Decimal Expansions:
    • Rational: Terminating or non-terminating recurring (repeating).
    • Irrational: Non-terminating, non-recurring (non-repeating).
  • Locating Irrational Numbers on the Number Line: Use geometric construction (e.g., Pythagoras theorem for √13).
  • Operations:
    • Sum/difference of a rational and an irrational is always irrational.
    • Product of a nonzero rational and an irrational is irrational.
